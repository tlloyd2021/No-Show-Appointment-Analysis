Introduction¶

The No-show appointment dataset has various characteristics of patients and their medical appointments in Brazil. This dataset includes information about a patient's conditions, including diabetes, hipertension, alcoholism, etc. Also, there is information regarding the appointment day,and other factors regarding the appointment, such as whether or not the patient received a sms and whether or not the patient actually showed up on the scheduled appointment day.

In this report, I will use the age, hipertension and sms received variables (independent) to explore the no-show variable (dependent). I would like to find what a no-show patient looks like in comparison to a patient that did show up.

Research Questions¶

What is the age distribution of a no-show patient vs a show patient? Were those that received a sms likely to show up for their appointment? Did patients with a hipertension condition show up to appointments more than those without the condition?

Conclusions¶

Overall, there were more patients that showed up for their appointments than those that did not. The average age of those that did show up for their appointments was about 38, which is roughly four years older than (34) those that did not show up. Hence, it seems that younger patients are more likely to not show up for their appointment.

Next, I analyzed the sms_received variable among no-show and show. I found that those that did not show up for their appointments were more likely to have received a sms, on average (.43 no_show vs .29 show). Moreover, among those that did not show up for their appointments were actually the patients that had a higher porportion of sms received.

Finally, I explored hipertension among patients and saw that there was a .17 average of no-show that had hipertension and .2 average of show that had hipertension. These values were quite similar. With further investigation, I found that among those that did not show up, a higher proportion had a hipertension condition.

Limitations¶

In the dataset, the neighborhood column is interesting and could have been helpful in this analysis. Instead of including only the neighborhood name, I could have used a column for 'distance traveled in miles'. This would have given numerical data that could have been used to test relationships between distance and no-show appointments.

Lastly, this dataset was made up of many coulmns that were binary values, making it challenging to dive deep into the analysis and create particular graphs.
